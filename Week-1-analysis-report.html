This experiment commenced on the 20 July 2020 with piglet 2549 and 2550. Two males born of the same litter at 1.6kgs and 1.44 kgs, respectively. Both had full neurobehavioral scores pre insult. 2549 had a stable 35-minute insult period characterized by a biphasic cardiovascular response and hypotensive time of x minutes, and an end pH of 7.02. 2550 experienced extreme acidosis during the insult identified at 20 minutes and confirmed at 22 minutes. This was followed by reperfusion at 24 minutes, the animal quickly went into cardiac arrest and was unable to be revived. Deeper investigation into why the animal was so extremely acidotic was likely due to uncontrolled CO2 levels in the blood. This observation was coupled with deviations in the electrolytes which likely contributed to cardiac arrest. By 3 hours post insult 2549s pH had returned to baseline. Several instances of cardiac arrythmia were reported between 12- and 30-hours post injury. Furthermore, there was a period of hyperglycemia (maximum 15mmol/L; normal range (3.2 - 8 mmol)). 2549s neurobehavioral recovery was slow and never returned to baseline. 72-hour EEG showed normal background activity with possible sleep wake cycling. No signs of seizure were noted during recovery or during intensive car monitoring. MRI revealed hyperintensities in the T2 map in the parasagittal watershed regions. No abnormalities were noted in the postmortem.

Recalibrate or replace the ETCO2 probe on bed 1 prior to our next experiment. Amend the protocol to reduce the glucose infusion to 2mmol/kg/hour at the commencement of hypothermia. Define half measures on the neurobehavioral scoresheet.
