Full manuscript under review at Developmental Neuroscience

Animals

Twenty-three piglets were used in this study. Two piglets were excluded before the administration of stem cells because they succumbed to the HI injury. A further five were excluded because they died before the experimental endpoint. No HHS animals in the HHS group died. This difference in survival was found to be statistically significant (see Figure 1). With all exclusions applied, there were four animals in the HHV (4 males) group and seven animals in the HHS group (3 males, 4 females). Original experimental design had included a SHAM group to serve as a healthy biological comparison, though in initial experiments, one out of two of the SHAM piglets died before the experimental endpoint and both experienced clinical seizures following anaesthetic recovery. Due to the uncertainty surrounding the SHAM cohort's health, both SHAM treated animals were excluded from analysis, and future experiments were discontinued. To address the need for a healthy biological comparison, brains from five healthy piglets (2 males, 3 females) were obtained and culled at P8.

HI injury was equivalent between groups

There were no significant differences between HI injured groups across bodyweight, brain weight, heart weight, liver weight, age at arrival, and cardiovascular measures (Table 1). No significant differences at any time point were observed across the measured heart rate and blood pressure metrics, as well as arterial blood pH, lactate concentration (lac), arterial base excess (ABE) and HCO3-, and glucose (Glu) concentration.

MR Spectra outcomes following HI insult

MR spectroscopy was measured and analysed from eight piglets, three from the HHV group and five from the HHS group. Due to the low group numbers and a true large outlier in the HHV group, it was inappropriate to use Welch's T-test and the Mann-Whitney U test was more appropriate. A significant difference was observed in NAA/Cho ratio (Mann–Whitney U = 10.5, n1 = 3, n2 = 7, P = 0.036 two-tailed; HHV, 1.450 [-0.6374, 1.947]; HHS, 1.947 [1.715, 2.392]). No significant differences were found for any other measured ratio (Figure 2). Out of the eight piglets that underwent 1H-MRS. Reliable 31P-MRS scans were only obtained for two pigs in the HHV group as respiration became unstable during the signal acquisition for the two other pigs in the HHV group, and they had to be removed from the scanner prior to completion of the acquisition.

MRI outcomes following HI injury

One piglet from each group was excluded due to motion artefact, leaving three piglets in the HHV group and seven piglets in the HHS group. Due to the low group numbers and a true large outlier in the HHV group, Welch's T-test was inappropriate and therefore the Mann-Whitney U test was used. There were no significant differences in the mean T2 relaxation time (Mann–Whitney U = 10, n1 = 4, n2 = 7, p = 0.53) or ADC value (Mann–Whitney U = 4, n1 = 4, n2 = 7, p = 0.11) between HHV (T2, 75.76 [12.33, 186.30]; ADC, 317.10 [80.24, 754.4] ) and HHS (T2 71.65 [68.28, 75.10]; ADC, 290.5[273.1, 312.2]) (Figure 3).

Neurobehavioural scores were imporved in stem cell treated piglets

Neurobehavioral scores were decreased from 22 ± 1 in the HHV group and 22.00 ± 1 in the HHS group to 6 ± 8 and 17 ± 4 respectively at two days post-insult. Neurobehavioral scores were significantly higher in the HHS group on day 3 (HHV: 9 ± 8 vs. HHS: 21 ± 1; adjusted p = 0.044)) and day 4 (HHV: 9 ± 8 vs. HHS: 21 ± 1; adjusted p = 0.0311). There were no significant differences at any other time point.

Neuropathology was imporved in stem cell treated piglets

Observations of reduced neuropathology in the HHS group were complimented with immunofluorescence analysis examining the numbers of mature neurons with NeuN (Figure 4 A-C), degenerating neurons with FJC (Figure 4 D-F), and apoptotic cells with cleaved-caspase 3 labelling (Figure 4 I-J). Exploratory analysis of these immunofluorescent data showed significant deviations from normality and sphericity that could not be accommodated by data transform. Therefore, the data were analysed using the Kruskal-Wallis test. A significant difference in numbers of NeuN positive cells were observed in FC between groups at p< 0.05 level for the three groups χ2 (2)=11.25, p=0.0002. Post-hoc comparisons indicate the median NeuN number in the P8 (979 [893, 1021]), was significantly different to the HHV group (703 [-53, 1163]; P8 vs HHV q=0.015) and the HHS group (849 [788, 874] ; P8 vs HHV q=0.19). Similarly, a significant difference between HHV and HHS group (HHV vs HHS, q=0.038) was found. No significant differences were observed in the PUT χ2 (2)=2.757, p=0.26. Significant differences were found in the number of degenerating neurons in the FC χ2 (2)=6.285, p=0.041. Post-hoc analysis showed P8 group (0 [0,0], mean rank=4.5) differed significantly with HHV group (26 [-21, 78], mean rank=10.75) and the HHS group (20 [2, 46], mean rank=10.07) (P8 vs HHV, q=0.057; P8 vs HHS, q=0.574). Furthermore, no significant differences were observed between the HHV and the HHS group (HHV vs HHS, q=0.84). No significant difference was observed in the putamen χ2 (2)=3.359, p=0.1753. No significant difference in the number of apoptotic cells were observed in the χ2 (2)=0.9274, p=0.656 or the putamen χ2 (2)=2.70, p=0.2774. There was no significant correlation between the number of NeuN positive cells and cleaved caspase-3 [ρ=-0.200, p=.4564], or NeuN and FJC [ρ=-.4701, p=0.0680]. Colocalization studies demonstrated that the cleaved caspase-3 positive cells did not colocalise with NeuN, Iba-1 or CD34, but predominantly colocalised with astrocytes.

Conclusion

Combination treatment of stem cells with hypothermia lead to improved neurological and neuropathological outcome measures following HI brain injury, compared with hypothermia alone. Neurological and neuropathological improvements were associated with a reduction in the activation of astrocytes and microglia. These data support the hypothesis that freshly-sorted placental stem cells boost hypothermic neuroprotection, mediated in part by a reduction in glial cell activation, but the chemical mediators of these morphological changes remain to be identified.
