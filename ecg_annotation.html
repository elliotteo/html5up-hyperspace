Heart rate variability analysis

Animal experiments are still underway and the database is not yet finalised. Code for each step in the analysis pipeline are provided in the links above

To analyse the variability in the HRV I wrote a MATLAB script to mark the R wave peaks in the ECG recording using  maximal overlap discrete wavelet transform (MODWT). I then calculate the instantaneous HR. I then feed this into an R script to perform necessary pre-processing. Time domain, frequency domain and non-linear analysis.

Nonlinear statistics

  • STFD.
  • Sample entropy: Measure of the complexity of a signal.
  • Maximal Lyapunov exponent: Rate of divergence of two nearby points in the phase space.
  • RQA: measure of the number and duration of the recurrences within the phase space trajectory calculated from the recurrence matrix.
The power law parameter is valuable to predict death after myocardial infarction. In other nonlinear statistics of HRV (SD1 and SD2 from the Poincaré plot as well as the short-term fractal scaling exponent α1) were associated with mortality after myocardial infarction.

Finally, the resulting database will be combined with neuropathology scores and used to train a series of supervised machine learning algorithms to predict brain outcome severity based on the HRV parameters.
