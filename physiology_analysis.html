Piglet physiology

Experimental preparation

Piglets were sedated with 2-4% isoflurane (Bayer, Pymble, NSW, AUS) via a facemask and a Goldman Vaporiser. Piglets were then anaesthetised with a 5mg/kg propofol (Diprivan 1%, AstraZeneca Pty Ltd., NSW, AUS) injection through a cannulated (24 gauge) ear vein. Intravenous anaesthesia was maintained with propofol (9mg/mL) / alfentanil (50µg/mL) (Rapifen, ICI Pharmaceuticals, VIC, AUS) throughout the experiment. Anaesthesia was delivered at a rate of 10mg/kg/h until intubation with a size 3.0 or 3.5 cuffed endotracheal tube. Anaesthesia was then increased to 20mg/kg/h for 15 minutes, reduced to 15mg/kg/h for 15 minutes, and reduced again to 10mg/kg/h for the duration of the hypoxic-ischaemic (HI) insult. Following the HI insult, anaesthesia was adjusted between 4 and 12mg/kg/h to maintain sedation until euthanasia. Where possible, anaesthesia was kept at the lowest possible dose to maintain light sedation. Anaesthesia was maintained for the duration of the experiments. All piglets received a prophylactic dose of cephalothin (0.2 mg/kg i.v), gentamicin (0.25 mg/kg i.v) and penicillin (12 mg/kg i.v) 16 hours post insult.

Brain injury protocol

Piglets were stabilised before the HI insult for a period of 90-120min following the anaesthesia induction. FiO2 was reduced from 21% (air) to 4% to trigger hypoxaemia. The insult severity was titrated by manipulating the FiO2 between 2-10% according to our decision tree (Figure 2). The primary goals of HI insult were to achieve low-amplitude EEG (< 5 µV for at least 25min), metabolic acidosis (pH < 7.1), systemic hypotension (mean umbilical artery pressure < 35mmHg for 10min), and arterial base excess < -12 . Ischaemia was achieved by triggering hypoxemic cardiovascular de-compensation and decrease in MABP below the lower limit of cerebral autoregulation between 35 - 45mmHg in the piglet. The HI insult was terminated by the return of FiO2 to 21%. This HI protocol is well established by our laboratory.
